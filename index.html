Essentially, is a staple Korean side dish that's present at almost every meal. It's fermented napa cabbage—or cucumber, mustard leaf, scallion, chive, radish... Some are spicier than others. Usually, certain types are reserved for certain seasons, or dishes. The ingredients can also hint at which region a family is from in Korea. For more, please visit Seung-a's website!

, which literally translates to hand taste, is a magical je ne sais quois deeply embedded in Korean cooking. It's the unique touch (✿◔‿◔)ɔ･ﾟ✧♥ someone brings to their cooking that makes you feel right at home.

I returned stateside in 2020 having survived two dark Stockholm winters—as in 15:00 sunsets. You can find "kimchi" on many menus there. Some taste good, but, uh, some don't. I wondered why. Since being back home, I remembered that my aunt's is the best in the world. I wanted to learn her recipe, and make a website to shed light on to save humanity from poorly fermented cabbage and homesickness!
